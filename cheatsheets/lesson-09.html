Lesson 9: Expressing Desire

The #1 Secret: Japanese is NOT Ego-Centric

English: "I like X" / "I want X" / "I understand X" (I do something to X)
Japanese: "X is pleasing" / "X is wanted" / "X is clear" (X does something to me)
Critical: Watch the が!
In these sentences, が marks the THING, not "I"!
コーヒーが好きだ = Coffee is the subject, not me!

好き - "Is Pleasing"

Pattern: Thing が 好きだ

私はコーヒーが好きだ

Literal: As for me, coffee is pleasing.

Natural: I like coffee.

分かる - "Is Understandable"

Pattern: Thing が 分かる

本が分かる

Literal: The book is understandable.

Natural: I understand the book.

ほしい - "Is Wanted"

Pattern: Thing が ほしい (adjective!)

子猫がほしい

Literal: A kitten is wanted.

Natural: I want a kitten.

たい - "Want to Do"

Pattern: Thing が Verb[い-stem]たい

クレープが食べたい

Literal: Crepes are eat-want-inducing.

Natural: I want to eat crepes.

When There's No Object

If no thing is mentioned, YOU become the subject!
With object (が marks object) Without object (∅が = I)
クレープが食べたい 食べたい
Crepes are want-inducing I want to eat

Talking About Others: がる

You cannot use ほしい/たい/怖い about others!
You can't know someone else's feelings - only their behavior.
Pattern: Person が Thing を [adj-stem]がる

さくらがケーキをほしがる

Sakura shows signs of wanting cake.

For self (adjective) For others (verb)
ケーキがほしい ケーキをほしがる
怖い 怖がる
食べたい 食べたがる
