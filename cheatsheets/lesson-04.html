Lesson 4: Tenses (Past, Present, Future)

The Three Tenses

Tense Form Example Meaning
Non-past Dictionary form 食べる Will eat / eats (sometimes)
Present continuous て-form + いる 食べている Is eating (right now)
Past た-form 食べた Ate

Non-Past Tense (Dictionary Form)

The dictionary form is non-past, not present! It usually means future or habitual.

犬が食べる

The dog will eat. (future - most common use)

Present Continuous (て + いる)

Ongoing action: Verb-て + いる

さくらが歩いている

Sakura is walking. (happening now)

犬が食べている

The dog is eating.

Past Tense (た-form)

Completed action: Verb-た

犬が食べた

The dog ate.

て and た attach the same way! Learn て-form → you know た-form.

Time Expressions

Type Particle Examples
Relative
(to today)		 None needed 明日、昨日、来週、去年
Absolute
(fixed point)		 に 火曜日に、六時に、七月に
Easy rule: Need "on/at/in" in English? Use に in Japanese!

明日ケーキを食べる

Tomorrow I will eat cake. (no "on" → no に)

火曜日にケーキを食べる

On Tuesday I will eat cake. ("on" → に)

Common Mistakes

Don't use dictionary form for "right now"!
✗ 食べる for "I am eating"
✓ 食べている for "I am eating"
Don't add に to relative time!
✗ 明日に行く
✓ 明日行く (tomorrow = relative)
