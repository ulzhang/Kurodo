Lesson 2: The Zero Pronoun & を Particle

The Invisible Carriage (Zero Pronoun)

Every sentence needs a が-marked subject. When you can't see it, it's the zero pronoun - invisible but always there.
Zero Pronoun: ∅が = invisible "I", "it", "he/she" based on context
You see Full sentence Meaning
Dollyだ ∅が Dollyだ I am Dolly (default = I)
うさぎだ！ ∅が うさぎだ！ It's a rabbit! (context = what we see)
土曜日だ ∅が 土曜日だ It's Saturday (context = today)

The Particle を (Object Marker)

を marks the object - the thing being acted upon. (Pronounced "o")
Basic Object: Subject が Object を Verb

私がケーキを食べる

I eat cake. (I = doer, cake = thing eaten)

Combining Zero Pronoun + を

ケーキを食べる

= ∅がケーキを食べる = (I) eat cake

本を読む

= ∅が本を読む = (I) read a book

が vs を - The Key Difference

Particle Marks Role Example
が Subject Who does the action 私が食べる
を Object What the action is done to ケーキを食べる

Common Mistakes

The zero pronoun is still there!
食べる is not incomplete - it has an invisible subject (∅が).
Don't confuse が and を!
が = doer (subject) | を = receiver (object)
犬がボールを食べる = The dog eats the ball
ボールが犬を食べる = The ball eats the dog (!)

Quick Reference

Pattern Example Translation
∅が noun だ 先生だ (I/it) am/is a teacher
∅が noun を verb 水を飲む (I) drink water
noun が noun を verb 猫が魚を食べる The cat eats fish
