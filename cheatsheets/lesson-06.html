Lesson 6: Adjectives as Modifiers

Four Ways to Modify Nouns

Any engine (verb, noun+だ, い-adjective) can become a modifier when placed before a noun.
Type As Sentence As Modifier Example
い-adjective ペンが赤い 赤いペン red pen
Verb 少女が歌った 歌った少女 the girl who sang
な-adjective 犬がやんちゃだ やんちゃな犬 naughty dog
Noun + の - ピンク色のドレス pink dress

い-Adjectives (Direct Modification)

Pattern: い-adjective + Noun (no change needed!)

赤いペンが小さい

The red pen is small.

Verbs as Modifiers

Pattern: Verb (any tense) + Noun

辞書を食べた犬

The dog who ate the dictionary

犬が食べた辞書

The dictionary that the dog ate

Entire verb phrases can modify nouns - this is very common in Japanese!

な-Adjectives (Adjectival Nouns)

Pattern: な-adjective + な + Noun
When だ connects to another word, it becomes な.
犬がやんちゃだ → やんちゃな犬

やんちゃな犬が寝ている

The naughty dog is sleeping.

The Particle の (Possession & Class)

Possession: Owner + の + Thing

さくらのドレス

Sakura's dress

Class/Type: Category + の + Thing

ピンク色のドレス

Pink dress (dress of the pink class)

うさぎのオスカー

Oscar the Rabbit (Oscar of the rabbit class)

な vs の - Quick Guide

Use な Use の
Adjectival nouns (な-adjectives) Regular nouns
きれいな、静かな、元気な ピンク色の、木の、日本の
Don't worry about memorizing which is which!
If you get it wrong, you'll still be understood. Learn naturally through exposure.
