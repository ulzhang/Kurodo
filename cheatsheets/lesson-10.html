Lesson 10: Potential Form (Conjugations)

Key Concept: Helper Verbs, Not Conjugation

Japanese doesn't have "conjugation" - it uses helper verbs attached to verb stems. The potential form attaches to the e-stem.

Forming the Potential

Godan Verbs: E-stem + る

Change the final う-sound to its え-row equivalent, then add る

Dictionary E-stem Potential Meaning
買う 買え 買える can buy
聞く 聞け 聞ける can hear
話す 話せ 話せる can speak
持つ 持て 持てる can hold
読む 読め 読める can read
Ichidan Verbs: Remove る + られる
Dictionary Potential Meaning
食べる 食べられる can eat
見る 見られる can see
Irregular Verbs
Dictionary Potential Note
する できる Completely different word (出来る = "come out")
来る 来られる Regular ichidan-style ending

Critical: が Marks What CAN BE Done

With potential form, が marks the thing that is do-able, not the person doing it. This is because potential describes a state!

本が読める

The book is readable. (NOT "I can read the book")

が marks 本 (book) = book is the subject!

私は本が読める

As for me, the book is readable. / To me, the book is readable.

Compare: Just like たい form (Lesson 9), the thing being wanted/possible takes が.

できる - The Potential of する

日本語ができる

"Japanese is possible" (not "I can do Japanese")

私は日本語ができる

To me, Japanese is possible.

Potential Form is an Ichidan Verb

The potential helper (even just る) creates a regular ichidan verb. Conjugate it like any other!
Form Example Meaning
Positive 歩ける can walk
Negative 歩けない can't walk
Past 歩けた could walk
Past Negative 歩けなかった couldn't walk
Form できる Meaning
Positive できる is possible
Negative できない isn't possible
Past できた was possible
Past Negative できなかった wasn't possible

Common Mistakes

Using を instead of が with potential
✗ 本を読める (treating "I" as subject)
✓ 本が読める (book is the subject = is readable)
Thinking potential = "I can do X"
The literal meaning is "X is do-able" - the focus is on X, not on you.
Forgetting できる is irregular
✗ しられる
✓ できる (completely different word for する potential)
