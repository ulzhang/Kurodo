Lesson 1: Core Sentence Structure

The Three Sentence Types

Every Japanese sentence has the same core: A + Engine. The engine determines the sentence type.

Type Engine Pattern Example
A does B (Verb) Ends in う-sound A が Verb さくらが歩く
A is B (Noun) だ A が B だ さくらが日本人だ
A is B (Adjective) Ends in い A が い-adj ペンが赤い

The Particle が

が is the center of Japanese grammar. It marks the subject - the doer or be-er of the sentence.
Subject Marker: A が [does/is something]

さくらが歩く

Sakura walks. (Sakura = the one who walks)

The Copula だ

A is B: A が B だ

さくらが日本人だ

Sakura is Japanese. (だ = equals sign, works one way only)

い-Adjectives

い-adjectives already contain "is" - no だ needed!

ペンが赤い

The pen is red. (赤い = "is-red", not just "red")

Common Mistakes

Don't add だ to い-adjectives!
✗ ペンが赤いだ
✓ ペンが赤い
Don't forget が marks the subject!
Every sentence needs a が-marked subject (even if invisible).

Quick Reference

Element Function Example
が Subject marker 犬が
だ Copula (A=B) 学生だ
Verb (う) Action engine 歩く、食べる
い-adj Description engine 赤い、大きい
