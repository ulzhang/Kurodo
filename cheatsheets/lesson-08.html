Lesson 8: に Particle (Extended) & へ Particle

に - The Target Particle

に nearly always marks a target of some kind.
Use What it marks Example
Destination Where you go TO 公園に行く
Location Where something IS 公園にいる
Purpose Why you go (goal) 買いに行く
Transformation What something becomes かえるになる
Indirect object Recipient of action さくらに投げる

1. Destination (Future Target)

公園に行く

I go to the park. (park = where I'm going)

2. Location (Past Target)

Location = past target. Something went there and still is there.

公園にいる

I am at the park. (animate beings)

本はテーブルの上にある

The book is on the table. (inanimate objects)

3. Purpose (Volitional Target)

Pattern: [Place]に [Verb い-stem]に 行く

お店に卵を買いに行く

I go to the shop to buy eggs.

(Two targets: shop = physical, buying = purpose)

The い-stem can act as a noun. 買い = "the act of buying"

4. Transformation (Becoming Target)

Pattern: [Thing]に なる = become [thing]

さくらはかえるになった

Sakura became a frog.

今年十八さいになる

This year I turn 18.

後で曇りになる

It will become cloudy later.

Adjectives + なる

Adjectives can't take に. Use く instead!

さくらが美しくなった

Sakura became beautiful. (美しい → 美しく)

The へ Particle - One-Trick Pony

へ (pronounced "e") ONLY marks destination of movement. Nothing else!
に can do へ can do
Destination ✓ Destination ✓
Location ✓ ✗
Purpose ✓ ✗
Transformation ✓ ✗
Indirect object ✓ ✗

東京へ行く = 東京に行く

Both mean "go to Tokyo" (destination only)
